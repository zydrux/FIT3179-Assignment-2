Global Health

This page uses data from the World Health Organisation (WHO) based on 2014. The aim of this page is to show illustration on how countries are comparable to each other.

Life Expectancy

This map is for you to look around at each country to have a look at data based on it. The luminance changes based on life expectancy, and the legend changes based on whether you want to see developing countries or developed countries. Countries that are gray are either not included in the data or arent apart of the selected country status.

Vaccinations

These charts show the percentages of vaccinations based of each country. The total expenditure refers to the percentage of total government money that goes towards health areas. The size of each bubble describes how many children under the age of 5 die per 1000 population. Luminance has no affect on the graph, the darker patches are just overlapping bubbles.

Thinness

This scatterplot is to allow you to observe the thinness thats observed in percentage kids aged fromn 5-9, against the percentage of thinness oberseved in kids aged from 10-19. The Human Development index refers to income composition of resources from 0 to 1.

Schooling

This map shows a heat map of average amount of years each country spends in schooling. The luminance changes based on amount of years, and the legend changes based on whether you want to see developing countries or developed countries. Countries that are gray are either not included in the data or arent apart of the selected country status. It can be noticed that the years in school and the map above about life expectancy have a visual relation that countries that have higher life expectancy, also happen to have higher years spent in school.
